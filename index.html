Introduction to Project

This project explores the development of a Reinforcement Learning agent for the game of Japanese Riichi Mahjong. We focus on designing effective reward structures, efficient state representations, and competitive self-play training regimes.

Introduction to Mahjong

Mahjong is a complex tile-based game blending strategy, probability, and psychology. Here, we provide an overview to help understand the environment in which our agent operates.

Rules

Riichi Mahjong is played with 136 tiles and four players. Players aim to form a complete hand (four sets and a pair) and declare "Mahjong" (winning) before others.

Tiles

The tiles are divided into suits (characters, bamboo, dots) and honor tiles (winds and dragons). Each suit has numbers 1-9.

Glossary

  • Riichi: Declaring ready hand.
  • Dora: Bonus tile giving extra points.
  • Tenpai: One tile away from winning.

Tenhou Platform

Tenhou (tenhou.net) is a popular online platform for playing Japanese Mahjong. It provides fast matchmaking and a competitive ladder system.

Results

Our agent achieves competitive performance against baseline heuristic agents. After 100,000 self-play games, it consistently maintains a positive expected point gain per game. Future improvements include faster simulation speeds and enhanced discard strategies.
